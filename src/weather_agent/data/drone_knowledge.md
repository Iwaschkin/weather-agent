## Wind and gusts

Gusts matter more than the average wind: a steady breeze is manageable, but sharp
gusts are what flip or destabilise a light drone. Fly with extra margin below the
manufacturer wind rating, especially for lightweight drones. Wind is stronger with
height, so a calm surface can hide strong wind at 100-500 m; climb cautiously and
watch how hard the drone fights to hold position. Always keep enough battery to
fight a headwind on the way home - flying out downwind is easy, returning upwind is
not.

## Cold weather and batteries

Cold sharply reduces lithium battery capacity and voltage, cutting flight time and
risking sudden voltage sag. Below about 5 C, expect noticeably shorter flights.
Keep spare batteries warm (inside a jacket) until use, and hover for 30-60 seconds
after take-off to warm the pack before climbing or accelerating. Never charge a
cold battery below 5 C. Condensation can form when a cold drone enters warm air.

## Rain and moisture

None of these drones are water-resistant. Treat any measurable precipitation, or a
high chance of it, as a no-fly. Even light drizzle or thick fog deposits moisture
on motors, electronics, and the lens. High humidity and flying through cloud can
cause condensation. If caught out, land immediately and dry the drone before the
next flight.

## FPV flying with the Avata 2

FPV drones are flown faster and more aggressively, so gusts bite harder than the
raw wind rating suggests. In windy conditions favour Normal mode over Manual, keep
speed down, and avoid flying far downwind. The goggles reduce situational awareness
of changing weather, so check the sky and a spotter before and during the flight.

## Low light, dusk and night

UK rules require unaided visual line of sight, which effectively means daylight.
The Mini 5 Pro tolerates lower light (LiDAR and strong sensors) better than the Neo
or Avata 2, but low light is still riskier: obstacle avoidance degrades and the
horizon is hard to judge. Plan to land before dusk, and remember golden-hour light
is brief.

## Geomagnetic activity and GPS

A geomagnetic storm (high Kp index) can degrade GNSS/GPS accuracy and disturb the
drone's compass, raising the risk of position drift, toilet-bowling, or a flyaway.
On high-Kp days, calibrate the compass away from metal, take off with a strong
satellite lock, and be ready to switch to a non-satellite (attitude) mode and fly
manually if the drone behaves oddly.

## DJI Neo specifics

The Neo is the lightest and most wind-sensitive of the three, with only downward
sensing and the lowest wind rating (Level 4, ~8 m/s). It is easily pushed around;
keep it close, low, and in light wind. Its short flight time leaves little margin
to fight wind home.

## DJI Mini 5 Pro specifics

The Mini 5 Pro is the best all-rounder: the highest wind rating (~12 m/s),
omnidirectional sensing with forward LiDAR, and the longest flight time. With the
standard battery it stays under 250 g (UK C0). The Intelligent Flight Battery Plus
adds endurance but pushes it over 250 g, changing its UK category and the distances
you must keep from people.

## Pre-flight checklist

Check the forecast for gusts, precipitation, temperature, and visibility across the
whole intended flight window, not just the start. Confirm airspace and any Flight
Restriction Zones (CAA Drone Assist / Altitude Angel). Update firmware, calibrate
the compass if prompted, start with full warm batteries, and confirm a strong
satellite lock before take-off. Set a sensible return-to-home altitude above local
obstacles.
